Printmaking

Exploring printmaking techniques and experimenting with colors.

Role: Art Maker, Student
Timeline: Summer Quarter

Process

I learned traditional printmaking techniques, from preparing copper plates to mixing inks and handling studio chemicals. By experimenting with plate wiping, inking methods, and test prints, I explored how different choices shape differnt images.

Etching

I began by preparing a copper plate and covering it with a ground layer, then carefully scratching out my design to reveal the metal beneath. After etching the plate in acid, I refined the details through multiple rounds of mark making. Finally, I inked and wiped the plate, printing the design onto paper.

Monotype

Monotype was the first printmaking technique I learned. It introduced me to the basics of inking, plate preparation, and the surprise element of pulling an unique print, it's really a exploration balance between control and experimentation.

Monotype + Lithography

The net texture on green background is made by monotype, the spiders and fish is made by a technique called paper lithography. The second prints is a guost print of the first one, making the net textures identical but opposite in colors. I really like paper lithography, I am able to catch tiny details in the image.

Edtion Seris

This print series explores the relationship between inner reflection and the external world. The meditating girl and her thought bubble act as a frame, it's made by an etching plate. Different scenes of background leaves flexibility for my creativity. The techniques I used includes monotype layers, viscosity rolls on the plate, and lithography details.
