Printmaking

Exploring printmaking techniques and experimenting with colors.

Role: Art Maker, Student
Timeline: Summer Quarter

Process

I learned traditional printmaking techniques, from preparing copper plates to mixing inks and handling studio chemicals. By experimenting with plate wiping, inking methods, and test prints, I explored how different choices shape differnt images.

Etching

Apply a ground layer to copper plate, then carefully scratching out design to reveal the metal. After etching the plate in acid, use drypoint to refined the details through mark making. Finally, I inked and wiped the plate, printing the design onto paper.

Monotype

Monotype was the first printmaking technique I learned. Ilearn the basics of inking, plate preparation, and the surprise elements of pulling an unique print, it's really a exploration balance between control and experimentation.

Monotype + Lithography

The net texture on green background is made by monotype, the spiders and fish is made by a technique called paper lithography. The second prints is a guost print of the first one, making the net textures identical but opposite in colors.

Edtion Seris

This print series explores the relationship between inner reflection and the external world. It's made by an etching plate and other techniques includes monotype layers, viscosity rolls on the plate, and lithography details.
