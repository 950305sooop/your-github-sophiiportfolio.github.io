Your Community

Residency verified app that helps apartment builds warm and supportive community between residents.

Role: UX Research, Wireframes, UI, Prototype
Timeline: July 18 — August 1, 2025
Type: Self-initiated project

Project Overview

UX/UI Design • Warmth • Figma

  • Tools: Figma, Notion, ChatGPT
  • Figma Prototype: YourComnunity Link

Your Community is inspired by my childhood in Yilan, Taiwan, where neighbors were like extended family. The app prioritizes trust, approachability, and low-pressure interactions, arm to help users build strong relationships.

The Problem

Modern apartment living often lacks the warmth and connection of traditional neighborhoods. Residents may live close by for years without meaningful interaction, leaving small but important needs, like watering plants, borrowing tools, or finding a workout partner unmet.

The Goal

• Encourages neighbors to help each other through simple, low-pressure requests.
• Fosters casual social connections through shared hobbies and community activities.

User Research

Interviewing neighbors, Competitive analysis of similar platforms

Four elements that stand out from the interview and analysis result:
Connection gaps, lack of close-by support, safety concerns, and importance of casual interaction for people who lives alone.

Competitive Audit

Current platforms fail to create a safe, connected apartment community,
Leaving a gap for an app that combines verified resident safety, help exchanges, social clubs, and pet‑friendly features in a warm, approachable way.

User Personas

Interviewing three residents varies in age and different life styles.

Anne Brown

“Mimi is my family, I want someone trustful to feed her while I am not in the town.”

  • Age: 63
  • Goal: Having a safe way to ask favors, and found a trusted person to feed her cat while she is not in town.
  • Pain: It’s hard to found trustworthy cat sitter. Her friends lives 20 minutes drive away.

Ken Rivera

“I work remotely. I enjoyed the flexibility, but Sometimes I feel isolated.”

  • Age: 31
  • Goal: Find low‑pressure ways to meet neighbors with similar interests.
  • Pain: Feels disconnected despite living in a large apartment building.

Kim

“I met a lot of good friends through my dog, Maple! I wish there's a way tp better organize the dog play date.”

  • Age: 28
  • Goal: Meet neighbors who shares interests in pets and casual activities, having close-by friends who to support each other's pets.
  • Pain: Hard to coordinate schedules for playdates.
